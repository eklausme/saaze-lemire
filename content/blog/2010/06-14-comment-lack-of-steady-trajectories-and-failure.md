<p>The expression &ldquo;you are the leading authority on one or two topics&rdquo; implies that the topic must be narrow enough. Nobody is the &ldquo;leading authority on Computer Science&rdquo; or the &ldquo;leading authority on Information Retrieval&rdquo;. You might be &ldquo;the leading authority on the application of Bayes Networks to ranking problems in Information Retrieval&rdquo;.</p>
<p>Presumably, the granularity level depends on the popularity of the field. If you are one of the last two remaining Physicists in the world, then maybe you could be the leading authority on Quantum Mechanics.</p>
<p>For very popular fields, you need to be very narrow to be an authority.</p>
